About

Scientist · Advocate · Builder

I'm an incoming MD candidate and a Georgetown University Global Health alumna. My work lives at the intersection of health equity, bridging rigorous medical science with the social realities that shape health, and expanding access to technologies and care for underserved communities.

“Service to others is the rent you pay for your room here on earth.” — Muhammad Ali

His life gave weight to these words. He refused to compromise his beliefs, (by refusing to participate in an unjust war) even when doing so meant losing his championship and facing public criticism. His words remind me that whatever room we occupy - however small or large - the simple fact of existing in this world carries an ethical obligation. The privileges we inherit (education, safety, opportunity) are not ends in themselves, but resources meant to be used in service of something larger than ourselves.

Beyond my medical curiosities and passion for bridging gaps in healthcare, I spend much of my time horseback riding, traveling, and painting. While science helps sustain life, the humanities, art, beauty, creativity, help us understand what it means to live it.

Research Projects

Field · Lab · Policy · Community
Field Research · 2023–2025

Antimicrobial Resistance in Chronic Wound Infections — Tanzania

As Principal Investigator at Tanga Regional Referral Hospital, I led a mixed-methods study identifying bacterial strains and antibiotic susceptibility patterns in chronic wound patients. I coordinated multilingual research teams, conducted Swahili-translated patient interviews, and performed quantitative analysis with Python and STATA. The work yielded two first-author publications and a 1st Place award at the JHU AMHP National Public Health Conference.

AMRField ResearchTanzania1st Author
Wet Lab · 2022–2023

Prenatal Neuroscience & Zika Virus — Children's National Hospital

Investigated genetic factors (mTOR, HEXB) modifying susceptibility to virally induced prenatal brain injury. Executed Zika Virus wet-lab experiments, qubit placenta DNA isolations, and built conceptual presentations for national conferences including Harvard's Undergraduate Research Association Conference and Children's National Research & Innovation Campus.

Systematic Review · 2024–2025

Healthcare Access for Migrant Workers in GCC Countries

Contributed to a systematic literature review on work-related health disparities affecting international labor migrants in Gulf Cooperation Council countries. Served as a double-screening reviewer, assisted with IRB protocol submission, and co-presented findings at the Medical and Health Humanities: Global Perspectives 2025 Conference in Doha, Qatar.

Systematic ReviewMigrant HealthGCCPublished
Policy Research · 2021–2022

COVID-19 Open Data & Policy Mapping — Nature Publication

Under Dr. Rebecca Katz — advisor to President Biden's COVID-19 Task Force — I conducted policy mapping, analysis, and coding for WHO regions across MENA. We informed open-data infrastructure used for pandemic response and were published as a co-authored paper in Nature (2023).
